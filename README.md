# A "Relationships" Framework

## Goals
The point was to make a framework for conceptualising intimacy that works outside existing frameworks which tend to be built on oppressive norms

## Basic Assumptions
- The subject(s) are sentient beings i.e. capable of thought, feelings, memory and imagination
- Intimacy is fundamentally a label that your mind constructs from relationship-related situations and sensations
- There is gradient of what is knowable to any given mind
   1. You know your thoughts and feelings best of all
   2. You know your bodily sensations less
   3. You know of the existences of others less
   4. You know of their behaviours less
   5. You know of the thoughts and feelings least
- Basically, anything that isn't your thoughts or feelings is a guess based on perceptions that could be wrong

## The Reasonable Paranoia Assumption
   - Because sentient beings can and do lie, their true thoughts and feelings are forever inaccessible to you.
   - Therefore personal intimacy (which we will discuss below) is for all intents and purposes built on "assuming benevolence" until proven otherwise

## The Paradox of Intimacy
- Imagine the following scenarios:
   - There is a Person A, Person B and God
   - God will reward Person A for befriending Person B but won't reward Person B for doing the same
   - God can read the thoughts and feelings of both Person A and Person B, so cheating is impossible
   - Person B can read the thoughts and feelings of Person A but Person A cannot read anybody's thoughts or feelings
   - Therefore, any attempt of Person A to befriend Person B will be coloured with thoughts of the reward, Person B will detect that, determine it is inauthentic and begin to depise Person A
   - The problem now is this: Any attempt to achieve the goal sabotages it, similar to trying to sleep or clear one's mind of all thoughts, any attempt to actively do any of these things will immediately sabotage itself, yet we manage to do it every single day, which means this problem has a solution

## Flow - The Resolution of the Paradox
- Flow occurs when the challenge presented by an interaction is exactly matched by the skill available to meet it, producing immediate feedback, a merged sense of action-and-perception, and — critically — the dissolution of self-monitoring.
- This last part is what solves the Paradox of Intimacy: Person A cannot be caught performing intimacy for the reward if Person A is not tracking the reward, and Person A stops tracking the reward when they are absorbed in matching, in real time, the challenge the interaction is presenting.
- Flow is path-specific — what counts as "challenge" differs by path, though not all pa:

### What Counts as a Challenge in Vanilla PPR
- non-verbal expression => the strength of the habitual suppression reflex relative to the strength of the fear
- safety I (physical/removable threats) => the difficulty of removing the source of threat
- safety II (intangible threats) => staying calm and focusing on the vulnerable, and accepting their attempts to find touch-based comfort 
- verbal disclosure I => how many interwoven causes must be explained and accurately tracked
- verbal disclosure II => how much the emotional pain is strong enough to disrupt narrative coherence itself (crying too hard to get anything out or withdrawing too much to speak)
- validation I => constructing something unstated from partial evidence — inference under uncertainty
- validation II => suppressing your own reflexes and staying regulated so you add or remove nothing
- asking => the difficulty of explaining exaclty what "help" is need i.e. the complexity of the physical stress
- giving => the objective difficulty of resolving the physical stressor being asked about while maintaing vulnerable's party autonomy (so nature of physical stressor matter too, not just difficulty)

### What Counts as a Challenge in Impersonal Intimacy
- Physical tasks => difficuty of co-ordinating one's role with those of other participants
- Mental tasks => whether your unforced predictions and reactions to the unfolding content land at the same tempo as the group's, without needing to perform or mask a mismatch
- Empty tasks => sustained presence/attention (sounds easy, but there is a reason wives keep complaining that their husbands don't see them)

### What Counts as a Challenge in Structured PPR
*depends on your role*
- **Facilitator**: balancing rules with mood
- **Speaker**: complexity of context and intensity of emotional pain
- **Audience**: sustaining restraint and self-regulation across the waiting period before your cued turn



## Paths to Intimacy
*The path depends loosely on the number of participants but mutiple paths can have a similar range*


### Perceived Partner Responsiveness (PPR or Vanilla PPR) [No. of participants => 2 < x < 3]
- This one is based on feeling safe, validated, and helped (without losing autonomy) due to the behaviours/speech of an other
- Its the most vulnerable to the Reasonable Paranoia Assumption and the Paradox of Intimacy
- It's best-case scenario is bi-directionality else it becomes limerence when it's one way
- It's most efficient between two people i.e. dyad though their is a case for increased complexity at N>2 participants at the cost of reduced challenge for each functional relation. The extra complexity introduced by adding more participants, provides challenge of its own and produces flow when the challenge just outstrips or lags behind the skil of the participants,

#### Functional Relations

##### Non-verbal expression <-> Safety (feeling safe)
- **Non-verbal expression**
   1. The expressions that matter for our model are expressions of fear (perception of threat from the outside the dyad whether another person, a situation or the enviroment)
   2. Ideally it must be expressed and not suppressed or hidden
   3. There is a unique case if the source of threat is the person the fear is being expressed to
   4. There are two kinds of threat
      - Physical/removable threats 
      - Intangible threats (anxiety, phobia, systemic oppression, abuse-related, imagined like from watching a scary movie or an anxiety-inducing movie)
- **Safety**
   1. Remove the source of threat - this usually works for minor physically easy-to-deal-with threats, where there is an obvious and easy solution
   2. Some sources of threat are not physical and are not easy-to-deal-with: systemic oppression, anxiety, phobias etc. Those ones are what the following tactics are for
      - accept nonverbal attempts to find comfort through touch (e.g. reaching for your hand or going for a hug) "most" of the time
      - make eye contact and remain physically still

##### Verbal disclosure <-> Validation (feeling validated)
- **Verbal disclosure**
   1. The disclosures that matter for our model are disclosures about loss, social pain, self-worth, or emotional (rather than physical) overwhelm
   2. Idealy accompanied by an explanation of the context
   3. It has two important seperate parts
      - intense emotions (might be singular or mixed)
      - complex context (series of events and actors that caused the emotions)
- **Validation**
   1. predicting unspoken causal patterns (not emotion) in their disclosure, if you needed, describe their history/biology or current circumstances to do so 
   2. reflect spoken fragments of intense emotion

##### Asking <-> Giving (feeling helped without violating autonomy)
- **Asking**
   1. The requests that matter for our model are requests for help with a source of intense physical stress (not a source of threat or loss, social pain, self-worth, or emotional overwhelm)
   2. Directness depends on how much stress they're under 
   3. and that perhaps explains why when what a person wants to request isn't about intense physical stress they tend to use "disguised mands" instead, very ineffective thing though, since it requires mind reading and guessing to respond to
   4. Disguised mands like these can be safely ignored, if they want something they should speak up or lock up
- **Giving**
   1. provide choice ("I trust you to make the right choice")
   2. acknowledge feelings and perspectives before offering help
   3. give rationale for suggestions
   4. offer flexibility on content, timing, and form
   5. check that your own motivation is self-endorsed, not obligated

#### Disguised Mands <-> Non-engagement
- **Disguised mands**
   1. Situation 1: There is a legitimate fear, emotional pain, or physical stress and instead of going to the usual channel listed above the person suppress non-verbal expression, avoids disclosure and refuses to ask instead using excessively subtle methods of communication. This is a failure on the part of the person, a failure to be vulnerable and just like if the other person were to fail to provide safety, validation of giving, it sabotages the intimacy process
   2. Situation 2: The person just wants something (e.g. sex, ice-cream, money [not for anything critical], car, game-buddy etc.), there is no source of threat, emotional pain or physical stress and rather than negotiate or just ask, they instead use excessively subtle hints and tips and disguise their desires. This is a failure of a different kind, it is not a critical faiure of the intimacy process because it does not even belong in the domain of intimacy in any meaningfuly direct way, it more belongs in the domain of negotiation
- **Non-engagement**
   1. This behaviour is a failure to use the appropriate channel and should not be reinforced, in order to encourage assertiveness or direct negotiation instead.



### Impersonal Intimacy/Interaction Ritual [No. of participants => from 2 upwards but less than a crowd]
- This one is based on mutual predictability within a shared script or structure
- Sentient beings are still required but they exist more as roles rather than individuals, making them more replaceable
- However, the roles must be willingly chosen, freely performed with an exit available, not enforced through any means direct or indirect in order to prevent compulsory role performance
- **Romantic Script** is an intersection of Cisheteropatriachy and Amatonormativity that tries to validate coercive gender roles as impersonal intimacy

#### Core mechanisms
- **Bodily copresense**: situation must be face-to-face
- **Mutual focus of attention (ritual)**
   - Physical task with interdependent complementary roles e.g. partner dance, multiplayer videogames, sports, giving/receiving a massage, 
   - Mental task with uniform roles e.g. following a narrative (film, book), solving a non-obligatory conceptual problem together
   - Empty tasks 
      - tasks that directly manufacture a shared mood without bothering wih co-ordination or communication - also these specifically require absense of scarcity to function properly
      - e.g. eating together (breakfast, lunch, dinner, snacks, dessert, fruits), drinking alcohol together, consuming substances together, sleeping together, smelling pleasant scents together, sharing an awe-inspiring sight (stars, sunset, architecture, art), sharing a meme, taking a walk, exercising together, being in a body of water (hot tub, pool, river) together, receiving a massage side-by-side, voyeuring together
- **Shared Mood/Emotion**: the roles must produce "flow" in all participants
- **Barriers to outsiders**: this one, rather than being about exclusion is more about skill gates, those too skill or too unskill aren't allowed to participate as they might ruin the flow.

### Structured PPR  [No. of participants => 2 < x < crowd]
- It exists to host reciprocal personal disclosure between two or more people as long as they are not a crowd
- e.g. AA meetings, catching up, joking around (sex jokes, inside jokes)
- The validation here happens through "reciprocal disclosure", the safety through the rules
- The core mechanism

#### Ritual
*each turn goes through the followng steps, except for intialisation which happens once at the beginning if the structured PPR is not a spontaneous event*
- **Intialisation** (optional): Facilitator explains the rules, intro, turns, outro and hands the floor to the first Speaker
- **Opening**: Speaker does the "intro" and links their turn to the shared context (prior speaker, inside joke, relationship history, group mood).
- **Body**: Speaker holds a preallocated, non-interruptible turn. Audience does not interrupt with Facilitator making sure of that. 
- **Closing**: Speaker executes a "outro" that marks the turn as complete.  
- **Transition**: Facilitator or group norm hands the floor to the next speaker without cross-examining, debating, or advising the previous one.


#### Core mechanisms
- **Bodily copresense**: situation must be face-to-face
- **Mutual focus of attention (ritual)**: a turn-taking conversation designed to be safe, validating and helpful without paternalism. Roles include:
   - **Facilitator**: handles turn taking. explains the mood, intro, outro. interrupts audience interruptions
   - **Speaker**: current Speaker
   - **Audience**: might listen. might laugh. might just watch. depends on the mood. but must never interrupt.
- **Shared Mood/Emotion**: the roles must produce "flow" in all participants
- **Barriers to outsiders**: this is because there are limits to how many people can participant before its no longer feasible for everyone to get a turn with running out of time.


## Relevant Oppressive Systems
*Note: This framework assumes all sentient beings are valid subjects. Therefore, we focus on systems that invalidate the forms intimacy can take, rather than the invalidate the subjects who can experience it, or sabotage the pathways to achieve it.*
- **Amatonormativity** invalidates any form of intimacy that isn't couple-form.
- **Kinship normativity** invalidates any intimacy that is not based on kinship ties.
- **Cisnormativity** invalidates any attempt to step outside assigned gender roles within intimacy.
- **Repronormativity** invalidates any intimacy that does not aim for reproduction.
